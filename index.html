you and the city

There are codes in Austin that shape your life. They can help or hurt you, cost you money or save you money, cause frustration or delight.

They shape more than just you.

These codes help or hurt our near and far neighbors, visitors, and the environment.

codes as in rules, not as in secrets

Zoning codes are city rules that tell developers what and how to build on land.

Zoning breaks land into pieces called districts.

Each district has rules that developers and citizens must follow.

It sounds simple, but the process is messy. There are winners and losers. Often, it's citizens like you who lose.

zoning strategies

single use zoning

Restricts a piece of land to one purpose such as a home or business.

goals

It aims to spread growth and separate buildings to reduce overcrowding, and increase public health and property values.

consequences

Single use zoning protected people and property in an industrial economy. But for Austin's service and high-tech economy, it's rules are ineffective. Large areas of Austin remain zoned this way.

Its rules often lead to car-dependent communities with lengthy car trips, traffic, dangerous streets, few public places, and harm to the environment.

overlay zoning

Integrates special zoning rules on top of existing rules.

goals

Overlay zoning offers flexibilty from the strict rules of single use zoning to achieve city or community goals.

Often it protects sensitive areas such as a historic part of town, but has a variety of uses.

incentive zoning

A type of zoning overlay that adjusts current zoning rules to balance conflicting desires of a community and a developer.

mixed use zoning

Allows land development for multiple uses such as combining homes and offices in the same building.

goals

It aims to reduce car dependency, traffic, and pollution while promoting close communities and a wide range of work and housing options.

consequences

Zoning codes focus on the use of land but often fail to control a building's form and relationship to its surroundings.

As a result the neighborhoods and cities develop without a person's experience in mind.

beyond zoning

form-based code

Regulates the form, size, and placement of buildings on land to standards that favor walkable and distinct communities.

Planners design the community`s form first.

People determine how to use the buildings.

form over use

Form-based code aims at predictable forms and less predictable uses rather than predictable uses and less predictable forms.

design principles

Ensures buildings are built for human scale and have distinct character while aligning with the social and environmental goals of mixed use zoning.

austin always changing

Zoning cannot fully control the form of the city. But it does provide a foundation for developers build on. The completed city is a negotiation between these two forces.

Austin and its rules are not set in stone. They can be changed. And we have a voice.
